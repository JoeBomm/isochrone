Example 5: Business Service Area Analysis

Scenario

A delivery business with multiple warehouses wants to visualize their 45-minute delivery zones.

Locations to Add (Los Angeles Area)

34.0522,-118.2437
34.1478,-118.1445
33.9716,-118.2473

Settings

  • Travel Time: 45 minutes
  • Travel Mode: Driving (Car)

Tips for Testing

  • Start with 2-3 locations to see clear visualizations
  • Use shorter time ranges (10-20 minutes) in dense urban areas
  • Use longer time ranges (30-60 minutes) in suburban or rural areas
  • Try different travel modes to see how they affect the reachable areas
  • Mix addresses and coordinates to test both input methods
  • Look for overlapping areas when analyzing multi-point accessibility

Understanding Results

  • Larger polygons: More area reachable (typically in less dense areas with highways)
  • Smaller polygons: Less area reachable (typically in dense urban areas)
  • Irregular shapes: Natural barriers (rivers, mountains) or road network limitations
  • Overlapping areas: Regions accessible from multiple starting points
